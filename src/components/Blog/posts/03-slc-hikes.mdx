Salt Lake City is one of the best cities in the US for hikes. Most only need a 15-30 minute drive to reach the trailhead, and then boom - national park views in your backyard. When I first moved here, it was kind of overwhelming choosing from them, so here's my personal progression of hikes and adventures to do near SLC, ordered from easiest to gnarliest. All of these (except for Fifth Water Hot Springs) are for the summer / fall, when there's no snow.

## 🧳 Introduction - Welcome to SLC!
You've just moved here, and from sea level too. It's tougher moving around at 4,000 feet! These are some good initial forays to explore and stretch the legs with beautiful views:

### H-Rock Overlook
Less than 15 minutes from Sugar House, this spot on the edge of the Wasatch foothills has an amazing view of the entire valley. It's a great place to give a dog a walk or get some steps in and some thoughts out. There's a short trail that leads to the top of H-Rock, a ~40ft. slab that with a giant H on it. There's also a pretty easy but slightly exposed scramble directly up the climber's left of the H. The area is connected to the Bonneville Shoreline trail, so you can turn it into a hell of a trail run if you continue south to the [Arcadia Heights Trailhead](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Arcadia+Heights+Trailhead/@40.7287591,-111.8100297,1560m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m15!1m8!3m7!1s0x8752603afe58dcc1:0x8c5c919e0897d88!2sSugar+House,+Salt+Lake+City,+UT!3b1!8m2!3d40.7126821!4d-111.853456!16zL20vMDNjN2s2!3m5!1s0x875261792513f047:0x8beb94bc6f513009!8m2!3d40.7272539!4d-111.8058782!16s%2Fg%2F11szg058bs?entry=ttu&g_ep=EgoyMDI2MDEyOC4wIKXMDSoASAFQAw%3D%3D). I usueally take that trail to [this overlook](https://maps.app.goo.gl/rAAAo2LuHzUDULh87), which is a good place for spotting how many places you recognize across the valley. 
\
Parking: [Option 1](https://maps.app.goo.gl/x6tm4jyz7kpsMSKc8),  [Option 2](https://maps.app.goo.gl/TZGrahnt2gRecWh57)

### Taking a lap around Sugar House Park
This isn't really a hike in nature, but it still makes for a great run with beautiful views and giant sweeping fields of grass. The park's proximity to the University of Utah and the city means that you'll see many others on the 1.5 mile asphalt loop around the park. An underground tunnel on the west side leads to Whole Foods and a bunch of chill remote work spots like the Sprague branch library, Hill's Kitchen, or Publik Coffee. It's also connected to the paved [Parley's Trail](https://www.parleystrail.org/map) which goes all the way from Mt. Olympus to State Street and beyond. Liberty Park has a similar thing going on, with lots of coffee spots, the 9-line trail, and a nearby weekly meetup where bikers take over the streets of SLC in the summer (aka '[the 9s](https://wasatchmag.com/a-night-at-ninth-and-ninth-at-nine/)').
\
Parking: There's tons of free parking inside of the park, but when I have a bike, I prefer [this spot on the east side](https://maps.app.goo.gl/KPE9XrEESqvHJn4P7) so I don't have to go drive through the entire loop to leave.

### Little Cottonwood - Secret Garden
The Secret Garden is the nearest decent boulder field from Salt Lake City. Given its location right at the mouth of Little Cottonwood Canyon, the views of the canyon's towers are just awe inspiring. At the east end of the park and ride lot is a trail that leads to the Alpenbock loop, a network of trails that hikers and trad climbers use to access the granite walls above. There's plenty more climbing deeper in the canyon, but this area is quite popular because of its easy access.
\
Parking: [LCC Park and Ride](https://maps.app.goo.gl/a78h9qSc94T4cuLF9)

### Donut Falls
This is the first actual hike on the list, and has the most nature in this category. It starts near the top of Big Cottonwood Canyon, so the air is fresher and about 10 degrees cooler than the valley floor's. That makes it great for the summer, when the sun stays out longer and the days are hot. The hike is only 3 miles round trip, not too steep, and very pretty - you begin in a large alpine meadow, and follow a meandering stream. If you want to see the cool donut-shaped waterfall, there's a mini challenge of scrambling and navigating the stream bed with some rock outcroppings at the very end. Just an FYI: taking a shower in it is frowned upon, since the water eventually becomes SLC's drinking water. This spot is also quite nice in the winter (if you have a car that can pass the winter driving restrictions), but the snow gets compacted into slippery ice from the high foot traffic. Shoe spikes definitely make it less treacherous.
\
Parking - [as of 2025](https://kutv.com/news/local/uinta-wasatch-cache-national-forest-introduce-parking-fees-for-some-recreational-areas-campground-picnic-areas-trailheads-public-comments-donut-falls) this area has a $10 parking fee, or just put your national parks pass on your dash.
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/donut-falls-trail)

## 🌲 First Hikes
If you want a bit more challenge and don't mind breaking a bit of a sweat, these excursions will likely take over an hour and give you an awesome workout at the same time. Trust me - they are well worth the effort. 

### Twin Lakes Resevoir
Even further up Big Cottonwood Canyon leads to Brighton ski resort. During the summer, it's pretty chill to park your car there for free, and explore the hiking loops around the alpine lakes. There are multiple resevoirs to check out, with the upper ones guarded by a steep but not-too-long leg tax.
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/twin-lakes)

### Ferguson Canyon View Point 
This hike is steep, but the views of the valley and canyon are amazing. You will probably meet other people and animals along this trail as you follow a stream bed under the shade of many trees, pass through a local crag popular for its granite sport and trad climbing, and finally do a steep hike up the mountain to reach a beautiful overlook. There's even an optional [15ft. boulder at the end](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ToRGZtf5RkMk2QaVA), if you so desire.
\
Parking: [This trailhead](https://maps.app.goo.gl/nKzjdHpaGviZ1Yix8) is the closest, but sometimes it fills up. You can also find ample parking [here](https://maps.app.goo.gl/pYnfGFxrGWRpx6Qx9), which adds another 10 minutes of uphill stairmaster through the neighborhood.
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/ferguson-canyon-view-point)
\
Fun Fact: This is also the start of the Wasatch Ultimate Ridgeline Linkup (WURL), a 32 mile epic that connects the ridgelines of Big and Little Cottonwood Canyon.

### 5th Water Hot Springs
These natural hot springs in Spanish Fork are the furthest drive on this list, but they are very beautiful, very popular on weekends, and most importantly, they are proper hot. To get to the hot springs, you have to do a gently ascending 2-3 mile hike. But if it's winter and snow has gathered, the road to the summer trailhead parking will be closed, which adds another 1.2 miles each way when you start from the winter parking. The trail also gets extremely slick and icy, so shoe spikes are very helpful for the steeper hills. There are over 20 pools, so there's plenty of space to find a spot to chill. The pools closer to the top (near a cold waterfall) are the hottest, and get progressively cooler as you go down. BTW, if you continue on the trail overlooking the waterfall for 1-2 minutes, you will find a fork that goes down and right. It leads to one more hot spring, which is more secluded. 

Also, on the drive in, you pass by [The Red Ledges](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iV3vs7PGJiirYMec7), which is another interesting spot for scrambling around, birding, and admiring crazy rock formations. There's a wide selection of campgrounds nearby (I've personally stayed at the [Three Forks Campground](https://maps.app.goo.gl/UsgeE56eDqQvmfrY8) before and it was nice). I've probably been to Fifth Water 10+ times since moving here - it's a pretty special spot.
\
Winter Parking: [parking lot](https://maps.app.goo.gl/R9yAcWkzAAnUDXgX8)
\
Summer Parking: [trailhead](https://maps.app.goo.gl/mXHN6UPWs6HhHEpS8)
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/fifth-water-hot-springs-trail)

### Jack's Mailbox
For those that want a workout: this longer hike up the Wasatch foothills near H-Rock is the first mini summit on this list. It always has stunning views of the valley and is usually secluded. There's a summit register in a mailbox at the top, which you can sign and leave easter eggs for future hikers. Because of it's proximity to Sugar House, this is a good one to squeeze in after work if you move quickly.
\
Parking: [trailhead](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Jyf4Cd3aMVGcJMbU9)
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/jacks-mountain) 

### Lake Blanche
SLC's #1 trail on AllTrails is at the top for a reason. A steep but rewarding climb leads to a unique valley with three lakes and Sundial Peak right below the Big Cottonwood Canyon ridgeline. It's an incredible view, and it's common to see moose up there. The length and steep vertical gain make this hike ideal for weekend trips or days where you have at least 2-4 hours of light available.
This was the first "hard" hike I did after moving here, so I didn't do much running on it my first time. But if you like hard trail runs, this is a fantastic trail for it. It's rocky enough to feel really technical, and running down is a blast. 
\
Parking: You can park at the [trailhead](https://maps.app.goo.gl/6L38z1ZcjkdQ2pue9), along the [side of the road at the S-bend](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Y8hU3BsP1JTsPwPx9), or in this [overflow lot](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JWCSuEpUC1tqt9K7A). Like Donut Falls, this area has a $10 parking fee, or just leave your National Parks Pass on the dash.
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/lake-blanche-trail)

### Bell's Canyon Lower Waterfall
The Bell's Canyon hikes at the mouth of Little Cottonwood Canyon are like Lake Blanche, but rockier and steeper. This hike is pretty popular as there are many destinations that share this trail: - the resevoir, the Lower Falls, climbing access to the Bell Towers, and the Upper Falls. I recommend the Lower Falls as a first destination. There are many sub-trails in between the resevoir and lower falls, so be sure to have sunlight if you don't want to get too lost.
\
Parking: I've always parked at the [Granite Trailhead](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8PCsKM9f56cmR9xp6) but sometimes the spots are limited, so another option is [here](https://maps.app.goo.gl/pPkrJvp5P7xMEPUGA)
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/bell-canyon-trail-to-lower-falls)
\
Fun Fact: this is the finish of the WURL, right after you descend from Lone Peak into Bell's Canyon.

### Honarable Mention: Hidden Peak
If you take the tram at Snowbird Ski Resort in Little Cottonwood Canyon, it deposits you at 10,992 ft. on top of Hidden Peak. But you don't want to pay the $70 to get up there - there's a better way, which is free! You can navigatre up using the trails in Peruvian Gulch, which has a dirt road that takes you to the top. There are many side trails that you can also take, like the Cirque Trail, which is near the end and allows you to gain a ridge with stunning views and reach Hidden Peak summit. There's a nice ski lodge with free water and bathrooms at the top, and if the tram is still operating (usually it stops running at 5pm, in my experience), you can also take it down for free!
\
Parking: I would park [here](https://maps.app.goo.gl/NREDTSRg6VxyQZF98) to reach the trailhead [here](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ZheiXzTtpbNKyqYz5)
\
AllTrails: [Link](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/peruvian-gulch-blackjack-trail)
